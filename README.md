# US Police Shootings Analysis (2015–2024)

## Overview

This project analyzes fatal police shootings in the United States between 2015 and 2024, based on data documented by [The Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/graphics/investigations/police-shootings-database/)

The objective is to examine demographic patterns, racial disparities, contextual factors (armed status, mental health indicators, body camera usage), and trends over time through statistical analysis and structured visual storytelling.

> The full analytical report and visual presentation are written in **Estonian**.

---

## Dataset

The dataset contains documented fatal police shooting cases in the United States from 2015–2024.

Key characteristics analyzed:
- Victim race (single-race cases only; multiracial cases excluded)
- Armed status
- Attempt to flee
- Mental health indicators
- Body camera usage
- Yearly trends

According to the dataset, U.S. police fatally shoot over **1,000 individuals per year on average**, with 2024 showing the highest number during the observed period.

---

## Key Findings

### Racial Disparities
- Black Americans are killed by police at more than **twice the rate** of White or Hispanic Americans, and six times more frequently than Asian Americans (relative to population share).
- Although roughly half of victims are White in absolute numbers, proportional analysis reveals significant racial disparities.



## Analytical Approach

The project combines:

- Descriptive statistical analysis  
- Proportional comparison (population-adjusted rates)  
- Data visualization and infographic design  
- Process flow modeling of police decision-making
